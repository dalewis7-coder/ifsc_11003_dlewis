Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Mars and the rovers. Have another question? Send it our way.

About Mars

Why is Mars red?

Mars looks red because its soil and dust contain iron oxide, which is essentially rust. When wind lifts this reddish dust into the thin atmosphere, it gives the Martian sky a pinkish color as well.

How far away is Mars from Earth?

The distance changes constantly as both planets orbit the Sun. At closest approach, Mars is about 54.6 million km away. At its farthest, it is over 400 million km. This variability is why Mars missions launch during specific windows every 26 months.

Does Mars have seasons?

Yes. Mars has four seasons like Earth because of a similar axial tilt of about 25 degrees. But since Mars takes 687 Earth days to orbit the Sun, each season lasts roughly twice as long as seasons on Earth.

Could Mars have supported life in the past?

Scientists think it is possible. Evidence shows Mars had liquid water billions of years ago. Curiosity confirmed that Gale Crater once held a freshwater lake with chemistry suitable for microbial life. Whether life actually existed there remains one of science's biggest open questions.

What is the temperature on Mars?

Mars is much colder than Earth on average. Temperatures range from about -125 degrees Celsius near the poles in winter to around 20 degrees Celsius at the equator during summer. The average is roughly -60 degrees Celsius.

About the Rovers

How do rovers communicate with Earth?

Rovers use NASA's Deep Space Network, a global system of large radio antennas in California, Spain, and Australia. They also relay data through Mars orbiters for higher bandwidth. Because Mars is so far away, signals take between 3 and 22 minutes one way.

How do rovers get their power?

Spirit and Opportunity used solar panels, which worked well but could be reduced by dust build-up and limited winter sunlight. Curiosity and Perseverance use a nuclear RTG, which converts heat from decaying plutonium into electricity. RTGs work day and night regardless of season or dust.

How fast can rovers drive?

Rovers are very slow compared to vehicles on Earth. Curiosity's top speed is about 0.14 km/h, though it typically moves slower during science operations. Perseverance can cover about 200 meters per Martian day using improved autonomous navigation. A normal walking pace on Earth is about 5 km/h.

What happened to Spirit and Opportunity?

Spirit became stuck in soft soil in 2009 and could not free itself. Contact was lost in March 2010. Opportunity survived until June 2018, when a global dust storm blocked its solar panels. NASA sent over 1,000 recovery commands before officially ending the mission in February 2019.

What is the Ingenuity helicopter?

Ingenuity is a small 1.8 kg helicopter that rode to Mars strapped to the belly of Perseverance. On April 19, 2021, it made the first powered flight on another planet. It was designed for five test flights but completed over 70 before its final landing in early 2024.

The Future of Mars Exploration

Will Perseverance's rock samples come back to Earth?

That is the goal of the Mars Sample Return campaign, a joint effort by NASA and the European Space Agency. Perseverance has been caching rock core samples since 2021. As of 2024, the mission's exact timeline was being revised due to engineering complexity and budget considerations.

Will humans ever go to Mars?

NASA and companies like SpaceX are actively working toward crewed Mars missions. The biggest challenges are the six-to-nine-month one-way trip, radiation exposure, and complete life support self-sufficiency. Most realistic timelines place a first human landing no earlier than the late 2030s or 2040s.

Where can I follow current rover news?

NASA's official site at mars.nasa.gov posts mission updates and raw image releases. Free public domain images are available at images.nasa.gov. JPL also posts regular updates on its social media channels.

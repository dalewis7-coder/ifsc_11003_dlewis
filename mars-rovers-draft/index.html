About the Mars Rover Program

NASA has sent rovers to Mars since 1997. Each rover has helped us learn more about the Red Planet. The program is run by the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL).

Curiosity

Curiosity landed in Gale Crater in 2012. It is nuclear powered and still active.

Launch date: November 26, 2011

Landing date: August 6, 2012

Perseverance

Perseverance landed in Jezero Crater in 2021. It is searching for signs of ancient life.

Launch date: July 30, 2020

Landing date: February 18, 2021

Spirit and Opportunity

Twin rovers launched in 2003. Designed for 90 days, they lasted years.

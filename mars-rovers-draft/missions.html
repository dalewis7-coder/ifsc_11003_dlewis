Mission History

This page covers the history of each Mars rover mission.

Curiosity

Mission Goals

Find out if Mars could support life. Study the geology and climate.

Launch and Landing

Launched: November 26, 2011

Landed: August 6, 2012 at Gale Crater

Used the sky crane landing system.

Major Discoveries

Found that Mars once had fresh water. Found organic molecules.

Design Features

Nuclear powered. About the size of a car. Has 17 cameras and a drill.

Perseverance

Mission Goals

Search for signs of ancient life. Collect rock samples for return to Earth.

Launch and Landing

Launched: July 30, 2020

Landed: February 18, 2021 at Jezero Crater

Major Discoveries

Made oxygen from the Martian atmosphere. Deployed the Ingenuity helicopter.

Design Features

Similar to Curiosity but with upgrades. Has 23 cameras. Also carries Ingenuity helicopter.

Spirit and Opportunity

Mission Goals

Search for evidence of past water on Mars.

Launch and Landing

Spirit launched June 10, 2003. Landed January 4, 2004 at Gusev Crater.

Opportunity launched July 7, 2003. Landed January 25, 2004 at Meridiani Planum.

Major Discoveries

Found evidence that liquid water existed on Mars in the past.

Design Features

Solar powered. Weighed 185 kg. Had cameras, spectrometers, and a rock abrasion tool.

Timeline

2004 - Spirit and Opportunity land

2021 - Perseverance lands
